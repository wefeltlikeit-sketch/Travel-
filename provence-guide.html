Fri May 29 — Lourmarin Loop
Bonnieux · Lourmarin · Ménerbes
▼
Day 2
Route

Gargas → Bonnieux (views + walk) → Lourmarin (market + lunch) → Ménerbes → return

Highlights
  • Bonnieux — perched village, panoramic views, cedar forest nearby. Great for lunch with a view (~15–20 min from Gargas)
  • Lourmarin market — Friday market: linens, pottery, clothing. Village is flat and easy to wander. Renaissance château; Camus buried here
  • Ménerbes — quiet, perched, artists' village (Picasso, Nicolas de Staël). Visit Maison de la Truffe et du Vin
Shopping
  • Lourmarin: curated boutiques, linens, ceramics, soaps
  • Ménerbes: local truffle and wine products
Drone
  • Vineyard rows between villages — roadside pull-offs
  • Layered valley landscapes heading south toward Lourmarin

Sat May 30 — Apt Market + Rustrel
Biggest market day · Colorado Provençal
▼
Day 3
★ Priority day. Apt market is one of the oldest & largest in France — 300+ stalls since the 12th century. Designated site remarquable du goût. Go early.
Route

Gargas → Apt (~10 min) → Rustrel (~20 min) → Saint-Saturnin-lès-Apt (optional)

Highlights
  • Apt market 8am–1pm: food, crafts, cheese, regional produce, textiles, baskets. Stock up on picnic supplies
  • Le Colorado Provençal (Rustrel) — former ochre quarries. Red/orange spires, miniature American Southwest feel. Spectacular cinematic drone territory
  • Saint-Saturnin-lès-Apt — quieter village if time permits
Shopping at Apt
  • Regional cheeses, salted butters, olives, lavender honey
  • Textiles, baskets, ceramics
Drone — Colorado Provençal
  • Launch from rural ochre landscape — outside any protected perimeter
  • Cinematic opportunity: red/orange formations + blue sky
  • Check AlphaTango — Rustrel is open but verify before flying
🛒 Apt Sat ★ 🚁 Colorado Provençal 🧀 Cheese + honey

Day 4

Gargas → L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue (~45 min) → Fontaine-de-Vaucluse (optional, ~15 min further)

Mon Jun 1 — Gordes + Sénanque
Iconic hilltop · Abbey · Goult
▼
Day 5
Go early. Gordes is the most-photographed village in Provence. Morning light + fewer buses = much better experience.
Route

Gargas → Gordes (~15–20 min) → Sénanque Abbey (~5 min from Gordes) → Goult (return loop)

Highlights
  • Gordes — stone houses cascading down hillside. Visit the château, wander cobbled streets, hit classic viewpoints
  • Abbaye de Sénanque — 12th-century Cistercian abbey. Lavender fields in front just beginning late May. Drone restricted over abbey; road approach offers iconic still shots
  • Goult — quiet "hidden gem" locals love. Less touristy, great atmosphere, small market on Thursdays (not today but worth the stop)
Drone — Gordes area
  • Rural viewpoints outside village perimeter — not crowded areas
  • Valley below Gordes: layered limestone + agriculture
  • Sénanque: road approach only for drone — abbey itself is restricted

What to Buy
▼
Food
  • Regional cheeses + salted butters (Apt)
  • Lavender honey — local varietal, different from supermarket
  • Olives, olive oil, tapenade
  • Herbes de Provence (loose, not packaged)
  • Candied fruits — Apt is famous for this
Crafts + Textiles
  • Provençal linens (tablecloths, napkins) — Lourmarin best
  • Ceramics + faïence pottery
  • Olivewood boards, utensils
  • Ochre-themed art + prints — Roussillon
  • Vintage prints, brocante objects — L'Isle
  • Soaps (savon de Marseille)
Specialty
  • Truffle products — Ménerbes (Maison de la Truffe et du Vin)
  • Local rosé wine from Luberon AOC
💡
Market Tips
▼
  • Bring cash — many stalls, especially small producers, don't take cards
  • Bring a tote bag or basket
  • Buy picnic supplies first (baguette, cheese, fruit) — markets clear by 1pm
  • Arrive by 9am for best selection and least crowds
  • Parking fills up fast at Apt and L'Isle — park early and walk in
  • May is manageable crowd-wise — not peak July/August

French Drone Rules — 2026
▼
  • VLOS required — visual line of sight at all times
  • 120m max altitude above ground level
  • AlphaTango — check geozones before every flight. Many Luberon villages restricted (historic monuments)
  • EU certificate — ensure digitally accessible on your phone
  • Open A1/A3 category for leisure — avoid flying over people, stay 150m from residential zones
  • No fly over crowds — markets, village centers, tourist overlooks
  • 2026 regs slightly eased for professional-category urban flights but stick to Open rules for this trip
Restricted areas: Village centers (historic monuments), Sénanque Abbey directly overhead, crowded market days in village squares.

📍 Navigate to Base
🚗
Driving + Parking
▼
  • Car is essential — Luberon is not walkable between villages
  • All major sites within 10–45 min of Gargas
  • D-roads (departmental) are narrow but well-marked; go slow
  • Arrive early at Gordes, Roussillon, L'Isle — parking fills up fast
  • Apt market Saturday: arrive by 8–8:30am, park on outskirts and walk in
  • Many village centers have paid parking lots at the edge — use them
  • Marseille airport (MRS) ~1 hr from Gargas

  • Roussillon: ~10 min

Food + Wine
▼
Provençal Staples
  • Ratatouille, bouillabaisse (Marseille), daube provençale (beef stew)
  • Tapenade, anchoïade, pistou
  • Goat cheese (chèvre) — local and excellent
  • Lavender honey — buy at markets
  • Rosé wine — Luberon AOC, Côtes du Rhône
Strategy
  • Market mornings: buy picnic supplies (baguette, cheese, fruit, olives)
  • Long shaded village-square lunches — the Provençal pace
  • Hotel dinners at base in the evening
  • Many villages have excellent terrace restaurants overlooking valleys
Low-Carb Finds at Markets
  • Charcuterie, rillettes, cured meats
  • Hard cheeses, fresh chèvre
  • Olives, tapenade, anchovies
  • Rotisserie chicken (rôtisserie stalls at every big market)
  • Fresh vegetables — tomatoes, courgettes, aubergines
☀️
Weather — Late May
▼
  • Warm but not stifling — ideal window (peak heat is July/August)
  • Fewer crowds than summer
  • Wildflowers + early lavender: lower-lying fields may show first blooms late May
  • Sénanque Abbey lavender: typically peaks mid-June to mid-July — may be just starting May 28–Jun 2
  • Morning light: soft and golden, ideal for photography
  • Afternoon: can be warm — good time to return to base
  • Mistral wind: occasional strong gusts — check wind before drone flights
Lavender timing: Don't guarantee full bloom at Sénanque for your dates. Fields at lower elevation (Plateau des Claparèdes) will show color earlier. Check locally on arrival.
